About the app

"Kaiju" have been an important part of my life for...well, almost my entire life. One of the first movies I ever watched was Godzilla: King of the Monsters (the 1956 version, mind you), and I fell in love. I still remember going to the library as a small child and checking out Ian Thorne's error-ridden book on the franchise. That's all we had at the time, though, so did I care? Absolutely not.

I "fell out of love" for a while, but after watching Legendary's take on the titular beast, I decided to revisit some of the films I had missed, such as Godzilla, Mothra, King Ghidorah: All-Out Monsters Attack, and it was as if I was a kid all over again. Once my son was born, and, like most boys, obsessed over dinosaurs, I knew it was time to introduce him to my childhood friend.

Over the past three or so years, however, I've definitely gravitated more towards the Ultraman franchise. No disrespect to Godzilla: he's a force to be reckoned with and all. However, there's just something to be said about a superhero show where the point isn't violence but compassion, understanding, and forgiveness. Fifty years later, Eiji Tsuburaya's Catholic fingerprints are still all over the show, and it's better for it.

Anyway, the origin of the app is nothing special. I'm a software engineer by profession, so I've been trying to find ways to improve/solidify my skills, and what better way than by building? This idea came to mind as I was thinking of how funny it'd be for someone to be pulled over by the police for not knowing what a speed limit of "1770 Godzillas Per Hour" meant. I mean, who knows what the next few years will bring, so better safe than sorry, right?

As for the actual application itself? For anyone who cares, I used Angular for the frontend work, and there is no backend API or database. I started with regular Bootstrap for some of the styling, but had to switch over to Angular Bootstrap since Angular and jQuery do not play well together.
